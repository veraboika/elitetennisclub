Tennis. What is it?

Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent or between two teams of two players each. The game has roots dating back to the 12th century in northern France, but the modern game as we know it originated in Birmingham, England in the late 19th century.

The sport is known for its prestigious tournaments such as Wimbledon, the US Open, the French Open, and the Australian Open. Tennis is enjoyed by millions of people worldwide and is appreciated for its strategic and physical demands.
